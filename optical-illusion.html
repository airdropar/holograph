How it works: The generator replaces the selected region with its complementary color (e.g. cyan for red, blue-green for orange) and overlays black scanlines. Your visual cortex's opponent-color channels adapt to the colored stripes and "hallucinate" the original hue — perceiving red even though no red pixel exists. This is related to the McCollough afterimage effect. Stripe density and ratio tune the strength of the illusion.
